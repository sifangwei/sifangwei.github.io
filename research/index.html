Research


Physiological and biophysical laws at cell surfaces

The cell membrane can be seen as a microscopic version of Manhattan, NYC. The cell packs the limited membrane space with proteins that provide essential functions. With such high packing density, the cell must evolve to do good job in “floorplanning” its surface: what parts are for powerplant (metabolism), transportation (nutrient uptake), public service (maintainence) etc.
Floorplanning strategies on the near-crowded cell membrane

Reorganization of cellular space; optimization of growth vs. adaptation time; different environmental limitations
