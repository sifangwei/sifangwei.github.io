Short Bio

I received a B. Sc. in Mechanics from Peking University in 2009. In 2015, I received a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Johns Hopkins where I studied the biomechanics of cell division and growth supervised by Prof. Sean Sun. Then I became a postdoc in Suckjoon Jun’s lab at UCSD studying bacterial physiology focusing on cell-size control. Currently, I am continuing my postdoc research in the Racki lab at Scripps Research to explore principles and mechanisms of cellular organizations using a pathogen, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, as a human health-relevant example.


We will be part of the Biophysics Initiative in the Department of Physics at Carnegie Mellon University.
